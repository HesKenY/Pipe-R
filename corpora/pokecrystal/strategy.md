# Pokemon Crystal — strategy corpus

Curated tips and decision rules. The compiler turns each block below into instruction/response training pairs.

## general_battle

- Switch advantage matters: switch in BEFORE a hit when typing flips. Switching costs a turn but immune/resist switch-ins are free turns.
- Status > raw damage early. Sleep, paralysis, and toxic shut down threats faster than chip damage.
- Burn halves physical attack — burn physical attackers (Machop line, Onix, Kingdra).
- Paralyze fast threats. 25% turn skip + speed cut wins more games than burn does.
- Confusion + attract is the trolliest combo; works on opposite gender.
- Always check the move's category in Gen 2: Fire/Water/Grass/Electric/Ice/Psychic/Dragon/Dark are SPECIAL; everything else is PHYSICAL. This means Crunch (Dark) uses Special Attack in Crystal.
- Self-KO moves (Self-Destruct, Explosion) bypass Sub but not Ghost-type immunity.
- Ground moves don't hit Flying or Levitate.
- Electric doesn't hit Ground (immune).
- Ghost is immune to Normal AND Fighting.
- Dark is immune to Psychic.
- Steel resists Ghost and Dark in Gen 2-5.

## item_priority

- Antidote: bag 5+ — the early routes have Caterpie/Weedle and Spinarak everywhere.
- Awakening: bag 3+ — Hypnosis is brutal mid-game (Morty, Chuck).
- Repel/Super Repel: stack for caves; one super repel = ~200 steps no encounters.
- Berry: holds, auto-eats at 1/3 HP. Give to fragile sweepers.
- Leftovers: 1/16 HP/turn passive. Single best held item Crystal-wide. Snorlax holds it as a wild encounter.
- Lucky Egg: 1.5x XP. Wild Chansey (Mt. Mortar) — catch enough for 50% chance, reuse on every team member.
- Amulet Coin: doubles trainer prize money. Hold it on lead during route grinding.
- Exp.Share: gives 50% xp to a holder, splits if multiple. NOT the gen6 party-wide version.

## mon_picks_johto

- Geodude (Dark Cave / Union Cave) — early Rock/Ground, demolishes Falkner + Surge later.
- Mareep (Route 32 grass) — solid Electric for Falkner-trail rest of Johto.
- Heracross (Azalea headbutt trees) — Fighting/Bug, Megahorn end-game wrecker.
- Gastly (Sprout Tower nights, Burned Tower) — covers Morty's own gym AND Sabrina later.
- Scyther (Bug Catching Contest at Goldenrod National Park, Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday) — speedy physical attacker.
- Eevee (gift from Bill in Goldenrod) — Espeon (day) or Umbreon (night) via friendship; both elite.
- Lapras (Union Cave Friday only, once per week) — Water/Ice carries Clair fight.
- Cyndaquil/Totodile/Chikorita — the three starters; can only pick one.
- Magnemite (Power Plant or Sudowoodo cave) — Steel/Electric — counters Sabrina + Whitney's Clefairy + everything that's bird/water.
- Snorlax (Route 11, behind Sudowoodo, wakes via Pokeflute radio card) — pure tank, learns Body Slam + Rest.

## hm_slaves

- HMs: Cut, Surf, Strength, Whirlpool, Flash, Waterfall, Rock Smash (TM in Crystal but acts like an HM).
- Don't put HMs on your sweepers. Build a dedicated HM mule.
- Bibarel doesn't exist in Gen 2. Use Sentret/Furret for Cut + Surf + Strength + Headbutt + Rock Smash + Flash. Furret learns Cut/Strength/Surf/Flash + has decent Speed.
- Quagsire (Wooper line) is the best Surf+Strength+Whirlpool slave with actual battle utility (Water/Ground typing).
- Mantine, Lapras, Suicune all valid Surf carriers if you want a real fighter.

## morty_gym_warning

- Gengar's Hypnosis + Dream Eater combo can stall you out. Always carry 3+ Awakenings.
- Bring Dark moves (Bite/Crunch). Houndour line OR keep Gastly from Sprout Tower as your own ghost (ghost > ghost 2x).
- Never lead with a Normal type — useless damage.

## whitney_miltank_solution

- Rollout doubles damage 5 turns then resets. If Miltank gets to turn 3+ on Rollout, it's a 1-shot.
- Miltank knows Milk Drink (50% heal). Keep PP pressure.
- Best counter: Machop (Goldenrod Underground gift) using Karate Chop. Get to L20 before challenging.
- Miltank is female; bring a male attacker with Attract for free turns.
- Geodude with Magnitude works (Rock resists Normal, Fighting type Magnitude super effective).
- Paralysis is gold — Mareep's Thundershock has chance to paralyze. Paralyzed Miltank can't keep Rollout chain rolling.

## clair_dragons_with_lapras

- Lapras (Friday Union Cave) learns Ice Beam from TM (Pryce gym gift).
- Ice Beam 2x vs all 4 of Clair's pokemon.
- Kingdra (Water/Dragon) only weak to Dragon-type moves. Lapras has none. Thunderbolt (TM 24) hits 2x as Water.
- Bring Full Restores; Kingdra's Smokescreen + Hyper Beam hurts a ton.

## kanto_route_order

- After E4: take ferry from New Bark to Vermilion (S.S. Aqua, sailor in Olivine).
- Crystal Kanto gym order is flexible. Common route: Lt. Surge (Vermilion, easy with Quagsire) → Erika (Celadon, easy with Pidgeot) → Sabrina (Saffron, ride Magnet Train) → Misty (Cerulean, return via Saffron after she returns from Cerulean Cape) → Janine (Fuchsia, via Magnet Train + Snorlax route) → Brock (Pewter) → Blaine (Cinnabar) → Blue (Viridian).
- Misty is missing from her gym at first; check Cerulean Cape first to trigger her return.
- Cinnabar is destroyed; Blaine moved to a tiny island (Seafoam side). Surf there.
- Blue's Viridian Gym is the last badge. Blue uses no theme, full coverage.

## red_mt_silver

- Mt. Silver entrance opens after all 16 badges. Red is at the summit floor.
- Red's Pikachu has Thunder + Quick Attack + Reflect + (varies). Can OHKO if you're <L70.
- Recommended: full L75+ team. Bring Snorlax (your own), an Electric resist (Donphan or anything Ground), and Ice Beam users for everything else.
- Heavy Ball helps catching nothing here — Red is a battle, not a catch.

## quirks_and_tricks

- Day/night: 6am-6pm day, 6pm-6am night. Different wild spawns. Set Pokegear time correctly at game start — fixing it later requires GS Ball glitch or Game Corner gimmick.
- Bug Catching Contest: Tue/Thu/Sat in National Park. Win = Sun Stone, Everstone, Gold Berry, etc. Always enter — it gives you 20 Park Balls + a guaranteed catch reward.
- Headbutt trees: smack them with Headbutt move to drop wild Pokemon. Heracross, Pineco, Aipom, Hoothoot all available.
- Mystery Egg from Mr. Pokemon (early plot) → Togepi.
- GS Ball event was disabled in Western releases, but Crystal includes a normal post-game Celebi event via VC Virtual Console rerelease.
- Eevee evolves to Espeon at high friendship during DAY, Umbreon at high friendship at NIGHT. Walk a lot, give haircuts, keep healthy.
- Tyrogue (gift from Mt. Mortar trainer at L10) evolves at L20 by stat: Atk>Def → Hitmonlee, Def>Atk → Hitmonchan, Atk=Def → Hitmontop.
- Pokerus is rare random chance after battles. Doubles EVs (stat exp) gained. If party member catches it, infect others by holding overnight in party.

## offline_safety

- Save before every gym leader fight.
- Save before every legendary encounter (Suicune, Entei, Raikou, Lugia, Ho-Oh).
- Beasts (Suicune/Entei/Raikou): Suicune is static. Entei + Raikou roam — use Mean Look, Sleep, low-HP throw.
- Lugia (Whirl Islands, Crystal): static, save first, throw Ultra/Heavy Balls.
- Ho-Oh (Tin Tower top): static, after 16 badges + Rainbow Wing.

## ai_loop_meta

- Text scrolling = mash B (advances text + won't re-trigger NPC dialog after end).
- Battle prompts = mash A (deterministic menus).
- Stuck overworld = try start, then b, then a (in that order) to escape menus, dialogs, or interact with terrain.
- Save state regularly via mGBA quick-save (F1) so the agent can rewind on bad decisions.
- Don't fight wild Pokemon below your party's average level by more than 5 — XP gain is heavily tilted toward equal-level encounters in Gen 2.
